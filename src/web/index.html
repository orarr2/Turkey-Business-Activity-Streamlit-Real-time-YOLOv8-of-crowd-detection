How the system decides something is suspicious

1. Extreme crowding - 50+ people in one view, or a weighted vehicle jam (bus/truck count ×2.5; threshold 38) - the top band of the 0-10 activity scale.
2. Camera blocked - a single confident object (≥45% certainty) covering at least half the view: something parked against, or held up to, the lens.
3. View gone dark - the image collapses from clearly lit to near-black between two samples: covered lens, power cut, tampering.
4. Returning visitor (events feed) - the same person/vehicle appearance seen again after ≥5 minutes away, at ≥96% visual similarity; buses and trains are not counted.
5. Prolonged presence (events feed) - a person staying ≥5 minutes or a vehicle ≥15 minutes in place in front of the camera.
Each alert type has a 30-minute per-camera cooldown so one stuck condition cannot flood the log. Routine ups and downs of traffic against the hourly norm are deliberately NOT alerts.

Is it getting better? - model mistake rate per tagging batch

Review detections - teach the system what it got wrong

Full frame with all detections overlaid. Click a box to cycle its verdict (grey → green correct → red wrong → grey), or use relabel to fix a wrong class. Click "Add missing" and drag on the canvas to mark an object the model failed to see. Every verdict goes to data/reviews.json, hot-reloads the collector's per-camera confidence within ~7 minutes, and feeds the training exporter (tools/export_labels.py). The strip below is a paced queue: the 5 frames the model is LEAST sure about, then the next 5 when you finish, with a breather after 30 - the full bank stays in the cloud, never dumped on you.
